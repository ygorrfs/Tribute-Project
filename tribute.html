Pixinguinha
Conductor of Brazilian Choro
Pixinguinha was a composer, arranger, flautist and saxophonist born in Rio de Janeiro
Time line of Pixiguinha's life:
  • 1897 Born in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil
  • 1912 Began to perform in cabarets and revues
  • 1914 Founded the group Caxangá with his friends João Pernambuco and Donga
  • 1919 Along with his brother China, Donga, João Pernambuco, and other prominent musicians, formed the revolutionary musical group Os Oito Batutas
  • 1921 First European tour sponsored by Arnaldo Guinle
  • 1920s Pixinguinha was hired by RCA Victor to lead the Orquestra Victor Brasileira, and during his tenure there he refined his skills as an arranger
  • 1928 and 1930 He released "Lamentos" and "Carinhoso". By the time, he was criticized for incorporating too much of a jazz sound into his work. Today these famous compositions have become a respected part of the choro canon
  • 1940s Joined Benedito Lacerda's band playing saxophone
  • 1973 Pixinguinha died in the Church of Nossa Senhora da Paz in Ipanema while attending a baptism The day which was believed to be his birthday, April 23, is today commemorated as the National Day of Choro in Brazil.
